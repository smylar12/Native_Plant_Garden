Native Plant Garden of Rancho Peñasquitos.

What is this garden?

This native plant garden in the Rancho Peñasquitos Preserve is an ongoing Girl Scout Gold Award project dedicated to restoring habitat, supporting local wildlife, and showcasing the beauty of San Diego’s native landscape. By planting species that naturally belong here, the garden strengthens biodiversity, conserves water, and serves as an educational resource for visitors.

Why Native?

San Diego is the most biodiverse county in the continental United States. This means it has more native plant and animal species than anywhere else in the lower 48, thanks to its unique mix of coastal, chaparral, mountain, and desert habitats, all packed into one region. Because this biodiversity is both rare and vulnerable, growing native plants is essential to preserving it. Native species sustain local wildlife, are uniquely adapted to the climate, and protect the habitat balance that makes San Diego one of the most ecologically significant places in the country.
